# Customer Journey Map for Quality-Guard

## Aware
- **Trigger Event**: Development team faces recurring quality issues leading to project delays.
- **Friction Points**: Lack of awareness of existing solutions; confusion about quality standards; overwhelmed by numerous tools.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration, confusion, urgency to find a solution.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Educational content (webinars, blogs) on quality management; free resources to assess current quality practices.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits and engagement with educational content.

## Consider
- **Trigger Event**: Team members start researching quality management solutions after recognizing the need for improvement.
- **Friction Points**: Overwhelmed by options; difficulty in comparing features and benefits; skepticism about effectiveness.
- **User Emotions**: Hopeful yet cautious; feeling of being lost in the options.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Clear comparison charts; customer testimonials and case studies; interactive demos.
- **Metric**: Number of demo requests and engagement with comparison materials.

## Try
- **Trigger Event**: Team decides to trial Quality-Guard after seeing its potential benefits.
- **Friction Points**: Initial setup challenges; learning curve for team members; integration issues with existing tools.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with anxiety; eagerness to see results.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Personalized onboarding support; easy-to-follow setup guides; responsive customer support.
- **Metric**: Trial conversion rate and user engagement during the trial period.

## Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: Team successfully integrates Quality-Guard into their workflow and sees improvement in quality standards.
- **Friction Points**: Resistance to change from some team members; potential underutilization of features.
- **User Emotions**: Satisfaction from improved quality; initial resistance turning into acceptance.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Regular check-ins from customer success teams; showcasing advanced features; success stories from other teams.
- **Metric**: User retention rate and feature utilization metrics.

## Expand
- **Trigger Event**: Team recognizes the value of Quality-Guard and considers expanding its use across more projects or departments.
- **Friction Points**: Budget constraints; uncertainty about scaling; potential integration with other tools.
- **User Emotions**: Confidence in the product; excitement about potential growth; concern over costs.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Flexible pricing plans; success metrics from expanded use cases; community forums for sharing best practices.
- **Metric**: Number of licenses purchased and expansion into new teams/projects.
